Lead-Acids

Lead-acid batteries are one of the oldest and most affordable rechargeable battery types. They offer reliable performance, low production cost, and high surge currents. However, they are heavy, have a shorter lifespan, and lower energy density compared to modern alternatives. Despite this, they remain widely used for backup power and starting engines.

Pros Cons
Low manufacturing cost Heavy and bulky
Easy to recycle Shorter lifespan
High power output for cranking RLower energy density
