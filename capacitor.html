Ultracapacitors

Ultracapacitors (also called supercapacitors) are energy storage devices that store energy electrostatically rather than chemically. They are known for extremely fast charging/discharging, long life cycles, and high power output—but they store less energy compared to batteries. They are ideal for applications requiring quick bursts of power or frequent cycling.

Pros Cons
Long cycle life Very low energy density
High power output Voltage drops linearly with discharge
Wide temperature tolerance More expensive
